How Much Does Social Media Management Cost in Dubai in 2026? (Full Breakdown)

May 29, 2026 · 768 words · Tagged: social media, Dubai pricing, marketing budget

Social media management in Dubai costs anywhere from AED 1,200 to AED 25,000 per month depending on who you hire and what is actually included. That is a useless answer, so this article breaks down the real ranges by hire type, the deliverables you should expect at each price point, and the costs nobody quotes on the proposal.

The fast answer (AED ranges, monthly)

  • Freelancer, junior: AED 1,500–3,000/mo for one platform, 8–12 posts.
  • Freelancer, senior: AED 4,000–8,000/mo for two platforms, 15–20 posts.
  • Boutique agency: AED 5,000–12,000/mo for two to three platforms, 20–30 posts.
  • Established Dubai agency: AED 15,000–30,000/mo for three to four platforms with full creative team.
  • In-house mid-level hire: AED 8,000–12,000/mo salary + AED 2,000–3,000/mo tools.
  • AI-led agency (Nova Foundation): AED 6,000/mo for four platforms, AI content, chatbot, email, reporting.

These are real 2026 Dubai numbers, pulled from active client conversations. They are not the inflated rates agencies post on LinkedIn.

What is actually included at each price point

AED 1,200–3,000/mo (entry tier)

At this range you get one platform, 4–12 posts per month, basic scheduling, and no engagement. Reels and Stories are usually extra. There is no analytics review beyond "did the post get likes." This works for a personal brand or a side business — not a real revenue-generating company. Nova's Launch tier (AED 1,200/mo) sits here and is intentionally minimal.

AED 3,000–6,000/mo (working tier)

This is where most Dubai SMBs land. Two to three platforms, 15–25 posts, basic Reels, weekly engagement, monthly analytics report. The quality of the content is the variable — at AED 3,000/mo from a freelancer it is usually template-driven, at AED 6,000/mo from an agency it should be brand-specific. Nova's Foundation tier (AED 6,000/mo) includes four platforms, AI-generated content at higher volume, plus a chatbot and email automation.

AED 8,000–15,000/mo (full-service tier)

Three to four platforms, original video shoots (not just Reels), influencer collaborations sourced for you, paid ads included or co-managed, dashboards. This is where most established F&B venues and beauty brands operate. At the lower end of this band, you are paying for one senior strategist plus a junior team; at the upper end you get a full account team.

AED 15,000–30,000/mo (premium tier)

Established agencies with named creative directors, custom photography and video, comprehensive paid ad management, PR coordination, monthly strategy meetings, and quarterly reviews. Worth it for hospitality, automotive, and real estate developers. Overkill for a salon or a single-location restaurant.

The hidden costs nobody quotes

The proposal price is rarely the real price. Here is what gets added on top:

  • Content production: If the agency does not include photography or video shoots, add AED 1,500–5,000/mo.
  • Ad spend: Management fees are separate from the actual money you spend on Meta and TikTok. Budget at least AED 3,000/mo of ad spend to make management worth it.
  • Tools and software: Hootsuite, Canva Pro, Later — AED 200–1,000/mo. Some agencies bill clients for this; some absorb it.
  • Boost budget for organic content: Many agencies "boost" your posts and bill back the cost.
  • Revisions beyond the included rounds: Senior creative shops bill AED 500–1,500 per extra revision round.

Always ask: "Is this price the all-in monthly cost, or are there line items I should expect on top?"

Freelancer vs agency vs in-house in Dubai

Freelancers are cheapest but break when they get sick, travel, or take other clients. An agency is more expensive but has continuity. In-house gives you full alignment with your business but costs 2x once you factor benefits, leave, and tools. The hybrid that works best for most SMBs is one in-house marketing coordinator plus an AI-led agency for production — the coordinator manages strategy and the agency executes at scale.
