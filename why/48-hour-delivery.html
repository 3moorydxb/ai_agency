Your chatbot goes live in 48 hours

Most Dubai agencies quote four to six weeks to ship a chatbot. We do it in two days. That is not a sales promise — it is what the build pipeline actually produces.

Pick any other agency in the UAE and ask them how long a chatbot takes to ship. The honest ones will quote four to six weeks. The dishonest ones will quote two weeks and deliver in eight. Either way, you spend more than a month waiting for a system that, in 2026, should be live the same week you sign.

Nova ships chatbots in 48 hours from signature to live. That is the standard, not a special case. Every Foundation, Growth, and Scale build follows the same pipeline, and every chatbot one-time build follows it too. The only thing that can stretch the timeline is the client side — if it takes you three days to send us your knowledge base, the clock starts when we get it.

How is this possible when other agencies need six weeks? Three reasons, all structural.

First, we build with Claude Code. Not a chat window where someone copies snippets back into their IDE — a real agentic coding environment where multiple AI workers operate on the codebase in parallel, write tests, run them, and ship clean code. The work that used to take a junior developer a week now takes a senior engineer one afternoon, because the engineer is supervising agents instead of typing every line.

Second, we built repeatable scaffolds for the systems we sell most often. Web chatbot, WhatsApp Business automation, booking system, RAG knowledge base — each one has a hardened starting point that already handles authentication, error states, message logging, escalation rules, and the boring infrastructure that eats most of the build time at other shops. We do not start from a blank file. We start from a working system and customize it to your business.

Third, our retainer pricing is pass-through, not padded. Most agencies bill setup hours separately from monthly retainer, so they have a direct financial incentive to drag the setup. We charge a fixed onboarding fee (AED 500 on Launch and Spark, AED 1,500 on Foundation and above) and the retainer starts the day the system is live. The faster we ship, the sooner you start paying for actual ongoing work — which is what we want, because that is the work that compounds.

The 48-hour promise is also a quality filter. A team that ships in 48 hours has to have its templates, prompts, integrations, hosting, and handoff rules locked down ahead of time. The agencies that quote six weeks are quoting six weeks because they figure each project out from scratch, and that means the result is fragile in a way they will not tell you.

If you sign with Nova on a Monday, your chatbot is answering customers by Wednesday. If that ever fails to be true, the onboarding fee is refunded. The standard is the standard.
