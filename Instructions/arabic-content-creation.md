# Arabic Content Creation

## What the client gets
All Nova content services delivered in modern standard Arabic and/or Khaleeji (Gulf) dialect — captions, blogs, ad copy, video scripts, and reply DMs. Targeted at GCC audiences (UAE, KSA, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman).

## What Worker does — step by step

1. **Dialect calibration (one-time) — Claude.ai.** Determine per client: MSA (formal/news/B2B), Khaleeji (UAE/KSA consumer), Egyptian (broad MENA reach), Levantine (Levant). Document choice in `BRAND_BIBLE.md` under Arabic Voice.

2. **Translation vs. transcreation — Claude.ai Project.** For every piece of content:
   > "Transcreate this English content for {{ARABIC_DIALECT}} speakers in {{GCC_COUNTRY}}. Do not translate literally. Adapt: idioms, cultural references, religious sensitivities (avoid alcohol, gambling, pork, suggestive imagery), local slang, and humor. Maintain {{CLIENT_NAME}}'s brand voice. Output in Arabic script with English back-translation in italics underneath each paragraph."

3. **Native review — human Arabic speaker required.** [NEEDS CLARIFICATION: Nova currently has no in-house Arabic native reviewer. Options: (a) Karl or partner reviews, (b) hire a freelance Arabic editor on Upwork/Khamsat for AED 50-100 per piece, (c) Omar's network. Confirm with Omar.] Worker sends the Arabic + back-translation to the reviewer with this brief: "Flag anything that reads as Google Translate, any cultural misfire, any awkward phrasing."

4. **Right-to-left formatting — Canva or Photoshop.** Arabic graphics must be RTL: right-aligned text, mirrored CTA arrows, Arabic numerals where appropriate. Use Arabic fonts: 29LT Bukra, Dubai Font, IBM Plex Sans Arabic.

5. **Hashtags + captions for Arabic platforms — Claude.ai.** Generate Arabic hashtags separately — Arabic tags have different conventions (less aggressive, more topical). For mixed-audience accounts, post bilingual: Arabic first, English second, line break between.

6. **Reply DMs in Arabic — Claude.ai Project.** Daily DM batch:
   > "Reply in {{DIALECT}} to each of these DMs in {{CLIENT_NAME}}'s voice. Use cultural greetings appropriately (السلام عليكم vs. مرحبا). Sign off in the dialect. Flag any messages you don't fully understand for human review with `[VERIFY]`."

## Tools used
- Claude.ai Project — transcreation, hashtag gen, DM replies
- Human Arabic reviewer — quality gate
- Canva / Photoshop — RTL graphics
- Arabic fonts — 29LT, Dubai Font, IBM Plex Sans Arabic

## Time required
- One-time dialect calibration: 1 hour
- Per piece: +30-50% time vs. English-only (transcreation + review loop)
- Reviewer cost: AED 50-100 per long-form piece, included in the retainer

## What to send the client
Same as English deliverables but in Arabic. Bilingual clients get both versions side-by-side in the calendar Doc.

## Quality check - CTO & COO review
- Native reviewer sign-off on every piece before publish
- No literal translations — every piece reads like it was written in Arabic
- No religious / cultural missteps
- RTL formatting correct everywhere
- Arabic numerals consistent (٠١٢٣ vs. 0123 — pick one per brand)
